What is DADA2?

DADA2 (Callahan et al., Nature Methods, 2016) is a statistical algorithm for high-resolution marker-gene amplicon sequencing. Unlike older clustering approaches that group sequences into Operational Taxonomic Units (OTUs) at a fixed identity threshold, DADA2 models the sequencing error process and infers the exact biological sequences present â€” called Amplicon Sequence Variants (ASVs).

ASVs are reproducible, comparable across studies, and resolve variation at the single-nucleotide level, making them the modern standard for 16S rRNA, ITS, and other amplicon workflows.
